### HS Codes, Declaration Records, and Shipment logs Explained

Understanding China Customs data begins with its core components. Every international shipment from China is meticulously recorded by the General Administration of Customs of China (GACC). These records include:

*   **HS Codes (Harmonized System Codes)**: These are internationally standardized numerical codes for classifying traded products. Each product has a specific HS Code, allowing us to precisely identify what a supplier is exporting. For instance, a supplier claiming to produce "consumer electronics" might, through customs data, reveal they only export "plastic casings" (a different HS Code entirely).
*   **Declaration Records**: These are the official documents submitted by exporters to customs, detailing the goods, quantity, value, origin, and destination. They are the bedrock of trade transparency.
*   **Shipment Logs**: These logs track individual consignments, including details like shipment date, port of loading, port of discharge, gross weight, net weight, and declared value. Analyzing these logs over time provides a dynamic picture of a supplier's operational consistency and scale.

### Filtering Out Trading Brokers using Shipment weight and value rules

One of the most common challenges for international buyers is distinguishing genuine manufacturers from trading companies. While trading companies can be valuable partners, they often add an extra layer of cost and communication. China customs data offers a powerful way to filter them out:

*   **Shipment Weight and Value Rules**: Manufacturers typically export large volumes of specific products, resulting in consistent, high-weight, and high-value shipments under a narrow range of HS Codes. Trading companies, conversely, often handle smaller, more diverse shipments across a broader range of HS Codes, reflecting their role in aggregating products from multiple factories. By setting minimum thresholds for shipment weight and declared value, and analyzing the consistency of these metrics over time, we can effectively identify and prioritize direct manufacturers.
*   **Consistent Product Focus**: A true factory will show repeated exports of the same HS Codes, indicating specialization. A trading company might show sporadic exports across many different, unrelated HS Codes.

This filtering process, which our GoCNScout database automates, is critical for cutting out unnecessary intermediaries and securing better pricing and direct communication channels.
