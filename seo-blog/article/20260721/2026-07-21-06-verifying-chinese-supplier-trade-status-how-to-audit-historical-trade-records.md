## Utilizing Shipping Registries to Perform China Supplier Background Check

![Utilizing Shipping Registries](/images/verifying-chinese-supplier-trade-status-how-to-audit-historical-trade-records-body-2.png)

The most reliable way to understand a supplier's true operational capacity and reliability is by scrutinizing their actual shipping records. These registries, often compiled from customs declarations, provide an unfiltered view of their export activities, offering a powerful tool for any comprehensive [china supplier background check](/blog/how-to-verify-china-exhibitors).

### Analyzing Shipment Frequency and Average Cargo Volumes

When we analyze shipping registries, we look beyond just the number of shipments. We meticulously examine the frequency of exports and the average cargo volumes over a sustained period. A supplier with consistent, regular shipments of similar volumes demonstrates stable production, reliable logistics, and a steady customer base. Conversely, erratic shipping patterns—long gaps between shipments, or wildly fluctuating volumes—can signal underlying issues. For example, a sudden drop in cargo volume might indicate a loss of major clients, production difficulties, or even a shift from manufacturing to merely acting as a trading company. We advise importers to look for at least 12-24 months of consistent data to establish a baseline. This data can often be cross-referenced with official customs data from agencies like the [U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP)](https://www.cbp.gov/), which provides aggregate trade statistics.

### Identifying Production Spikes or Capacity Drops

Beyond consistency, identifying significant production spikes or capacity drops is crucial. A sudden, unexplained spike in exports might suggest a one-off large order that stretched the factory's capacity, potentially leading to quality compromises on subsequent orders. Conversely, a sharp and sustained drop in export activity is a major red flag, indicating potential financial distress, labor shortages, or a decline in market demand for their products. In our experience, a client once overlooked a supplier's declining export trend, only to face severe delays and quality issues when their order coincided with the factory's struggle to maintain production lines. This highlights the importance of proactive monitoring of trade data as part of your ongoing supplier relationship management.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What indicates a dormant trade status?

A dormant trade status is typically indicated by a significant lack of export activity over an extended period, usually 6-12 months or more, within a supplier's historical shipping records. This can manifest as zero shipments, or extremely sporadic and low-volume exports compared to their historical average or industry benchmarks. It suggests the company may not be actively engaged in international trade, potentially due to financial issues, production halts, or a shift in business strategy.

### Can trading companies fake shipping records?

While it's challenging for trading companies to completely "fake" official customs shipping records, they can sometimes mislead buyers by presenting records from other factories or by exaggerating their direct manufacturing involvement. This is why it's crucial to cross-reference shipping data with other verification methods, such as factory audits and business license checks.
