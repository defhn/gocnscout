### Sourcing Checklist: Checking Incoming Materials and Output Quality

A critical component of any factory audit is the detailed examination of material flow and quality checkpoints. Our template includes specific sections that act as a sourcing checklist, guiding your team to:

  - **Incoming Material Inspection (IQC)**: Verify the supplier's process for inspecting raw materials upon arrival. Are they checking for quantity, quality, and compliance with specifications? Are records maintained?

  - **In-Process Quality Control (IPQC)**: Observe production lines to ensure quality checks are performed at critical stages. Look for evidence of calibration records for testing equipment and operator training.

  - **Final Product Inspection (FQC)**: Review their final inspection procedures, packaging standards, and pre-shipment checks. This is where Incoterms like FOB or EXW become relevant, as the point of inspection often dictates liability.

We've seen cases where factories claim robust IQC, but a physical audit reveals raw materials are simply moved directly to production without proper verification, leading to downstream quality issues. This checklist helps prevent such oversights, ensuring your team focuses on actionable data points.
