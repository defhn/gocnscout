### Factory vs. Trading Broker: Why the Distinction Dictates Margins

The core difference between a factory and a trading company lies in their operational structure and value proposition. A factory directly manufactures goods, offering lower prices, greater customization flexibility, and direct quality control. Trading companies, while sometimes offering convenience, act as middlemen, adding their markup and often obscuring the true production source. This distinction is crucial for your bottom line. In our sourcing experience, a client in 2026 discovered they were paying 15% more for a standard product because they were unknowingly dealing with a trading company that simply repackaged goods from a local factory.

### The Pitfalls of Using Outdated Static PDFs to Search Factories

Many traditional methods for finding suppliers, such as relying on static PDF directories or old trade show lists, are inherently flawed. These resources quickly become outdated, with contact information expiring, companies changing their business scope, or even ceasing operations. This leads to wasted time, failed communication attempts, and a high risk of connecting with non-existent or irrelevant entities. A static [china factory directory](/reports) from 2024, for instance, would be largely irrelevant for sourcing needs in 2026, missing new entrants and failing to reflect current export activities.
