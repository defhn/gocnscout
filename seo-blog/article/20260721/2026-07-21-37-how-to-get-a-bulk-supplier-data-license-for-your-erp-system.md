### Essential Fields: DUNS numbers, Tax Codes, Port of Departures

A truly valuable bulk supplier data license goes beyond basic company names and addresses. It must provide a rich array of essential fields crucial for comprehensive due diligence and risk assessment. Key identifiers include:

*   **DUNS Numbers**: The Data Universal Numbering System, a unique nine-digit identifier for businesses, is vital for global business identification and credit assessment. You can learn more about its importance from official sources like [Dun & Bradstreet](https://www.dnb.com/duns-number.html).
*   **Tax Codes/Business Registration Numbers**: These legal identifiers confirm a company's official registration status and tax compliance in its home country, crucial for international trade agreements and avoiding legal complications.
*   **Port of Departures/Arrivals**: Detailed shipping records, including the specific ports used, offer invaluable insights into a supplier's logistical capabilities, preferred shipping routes, and actual export volume.
*   **HS Codes (Harmonized System Codes)**: Understanding the specific HS Codes a supplier uses for their exports helps verify their product specialization and ensures accurate tariff classification, aligning with international trade standards set by organizations like the [World Customs Organization](https://www.wcoomd.org/).

These data points, when integrated, provide a holistic view of a supplier's operational footprint and financial health, enabling proactive risk management and informed decision-making.
