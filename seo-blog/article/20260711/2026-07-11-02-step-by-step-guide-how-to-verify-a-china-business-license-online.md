## The Legal Foundation of Sourcing: Verify China Business License

The business license (营业执照, *yíngyè zhízhào*) is the single most important document for any company operating legally in mainland China. It's issued by the Administration for Market Regulation (AMR), formerly known as the Administration for Industry and Commerce (AIC). This document serves as official proof of a company's legal existence and its authorized business activities. Without a valid, current business license, a company cannot legally operate, sign contracts, or conduct export trade. For international buyers, verifying this document is the absolute minimum standard for due diligence.

![Section 1 illustration](/images/step-by-step-guide-how-to-verify-a-china-business-license-online-body-1.png)

### What Does a Genuine China Business License Look Like?

A genuine Chinese business license is a single, laminated document, typically A4 size, featuring a distinctive red official seal (公章, *gōngzhāng*) of the issuing authority. It contains crucial information that must be cross-referenced with official records. While the design might see minor updates, the core data points remain consistent. Understanding these elements is key to effective verification.

### Key Registration Parameters to Inspect in 2026

When you receive a copy of a Chinese business license, whether physical or digital, several critical parameters demand your immediate attention. These details provide a snapshot of the company's legal standing and operational scope.

*   **Unified Social Credit Code (统一社会信用代码)**: This 18-digit alphanumeric code is unique to each entity and acts as a universal identifier, replacing older registration numbers. It's crucial for all official checks.
*   **Company Name (公司名称)**: The official Chinese name is the only legally binding name. Any English name is merely a translation and holds no legal weight in China.
*   **Type of Enterprise (公司类型)**: This specifies the legal structure, e.g., Limited Liability Company (有限责任公司), Joint Stock Company (股份有限公司), or Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise (外商独资企业).
*   **Legal Representative (法定代表人)**: The individual legally authorized to act on behalf of the company.
*   **Registered Capital (注册资本)**: The amount of capital registered with the government. While not always indicative of financial health, a very low registered capital for a large-scale manufacturer can be a red flag.
*   **Date of Establishment (成立日期)**: When the company was officially registered.
*   **Business Scope (经营范围)**: A detailed list of approved business activities. This is vital to ensure the supplier is legally permitted to manufacture or export the products you intend to purchase.
*   **Term of Operation (营业期限)**: The validity period of the license. An expired license means the company is not legally operating.
*   **Address (住所)**: The official registered address. This should ideally match the factory address if you are dealing directly with a manufacturer.

**Pro Tip**: Always request a high-resolution scan of the *original* business license. Low-quality copies can obscure details, making verification difficult.

## Vetting the Official Registry Online to Verify Chinese Manufacturer Online

The most reliable way to **verify a China business license** is through the official government database. This direct approach bypasses potential fraud and provides real-time, authoritative information.

![Section 2 illustration](/images/step-by-step-guide-how-to-verify-a-china-business-license-online-body-2.png)

### How to Navigate the NECIPS Government Database

The National Enterprise Credit Information Public System (国家企业信用信息公示系统, *Guójiā Qǐyè Xìnyòng Gōngshì Xìtǒng*, or GSXT/NECIPS) is the official portal for checking company registrations in China. While primarily in Chinese, its interface is relatively straightforward for basic searches.

1.  **Access the Portal**: Visit the official NECIPS website. (Note: The official website is `www.gsxt.gov.cn`).
2.  **Input Company Name/USCC**: Enter the company's official Chinese name or its Unified Social Credit Code into the search bar. **Crucially, you must use the exact Chinese characters.**
3.  **Captcha Verification**: Complete the simple captcha to proceed.
4.  **Review Results**: The system will display a list of companies. Click on the correct entry to view detailed registration information, including all the parameters mentioned above.

### Deciphering the Business Scope (经营范围) Constraints

The "Business Scope" on a Chinese business license is not merely a descriptive field; it's a legal boundary. A company is only permitted to conduct activities explicitly listed within its scope. For example, if a company's business scope only includes "domestic sales of plastic products" and you are trying to purchase "electronic components for export," this is a significant red flag.

We have seen cases where suppliers claim to be manufacturers but their business scope only covers "trading" or "consulting." This indicates they are likely a trading company or an agent, not the direct factory. Understanding these constraints helps you avoid misrepresentation and ensures your supplier is legally authorized for the transactions you intend.

## Detecting Forged, Altered, or Expired Business Licenses

Even with official checks, vigilance is paramount. Sophisticated fraudsters can present convincing but ultimately fake or manipulated documents. Knowing the common red flags can save you from significant losses.

![Section 3 illustration](/images/step-by-step-guide-how-to-verify-a-china-business-license-online-body-3.png)

### Spotting Discrepancies in Registered Capital and Address

One common tactic involves altering key details on a legitimate license copy. Always cross-reference the registered capital and address on the provided document with the information found on the NECIPS.

*   **Registered Capital Discrepancies**: A sourcing case we reviewed involved a buyer who lost approximately $50,000 after failing to verify the supplier's registered capital. The supplier presented a business license showing 10 million RMB in registered capital, but a check on NECIPS revealed only 500,000 RMB. This inflated figure was used to project a false sense of financial stability. *(Based on anonymized supplier cases reviewed by the auditing team. Details have been changed for privacy.)*
*   **Address Mismatches**: Be wary if the registered address on the license doesn't match the factory address you've been given, or if the address is a virtual office or residential building for a supposed manufacturing entity. While some companies have separate registered and operational addresses, significant discrepancies warrant further investigation, potentially requiring a factory audit.

**Pro Tip / Warning**: Always be suspicious of low-resolution scans or documents that appear to have been digitally altered (e.g., inconsistent fonts, misaligned text, or blurry sections around key data points).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How to check a China business license?

The primary method to check a China business license online in 2026 is through the National Enterprise Credit Information Public System (NECIPS), also known as GSXT, at `www.gsxt.gov.cn`. You will need the company's official Chinese name or its Unified Social Credit Code (USCC) to perform a search. This government portal provides official, real-time registration details, including the company's legal status, business scope, and registered capital. For a more comprehensive check, platforms like GoCNScout integrate this data with export records and legal risk assessments.

### What is the Chinese version of LLC?

The most common Chinese version of an LLC (Limited Liability Company) is a **有限责任公司 (Yǒuxiàn Zérèn Gōngsī)**. This legal structure is widely adopted by both domestic and foreign-invested enterprises in China. It means that the shareholders' liability is limited to the amount of capital they have contributed to the company. When you **verify a China business license**, checking the "Type of Enterprise" (公司类型) field will confirm if it is indeed a Limited Liability Company.

### How to verify the legitimacy of a foreign company?

To verify the legitimacy of a foreign company, especially a Chinese one, a multi-faceted approach is essential. Beyond checking their business license on the NECIPS, you should:
1.  **Cross-reference information**: Ensure consistency across all documents (license, bank details, website).
2.  **Check export records**: Use trade intelligence platforms like GoCNScout to verify their actual export history and volumes.
3.  **Conduct factory audits**: For critical suppliers, arrange a third-party audit to confirm physical presence and production capabilities.
4.  **Review legal standing**: Look for any public records of lawsuits or administrative penalties.
5.  **Verify bank accounts**: Ensure the beneficiary account is a corporate account matching the registered company name, not a personal account.
This comprehensive due diligence helps mitigate risks and ensures you are dealing with a legitimate and capable partner.
