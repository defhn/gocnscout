Common Red Flags of Unverified Chinese Exporters

During our sourcing experience, we have compiled a list of warning signs that indicate a supplier may not be legitimate. These include:

  • Inconsistent business addresses – The address on the contract does not match the one on the business license or Google Maps.
  • Refusal to share a business license – A legitimate factory will always provide a copy of its Yingye Zhizhao (营业执照).
  • Pressure to pay via personal accounts – Insisting on wire transfers to an individual’s bank account instead of a corporate account is a major red flag.
  • No verifiable export history – The supplier cannot provide past Bill of Lading (B/L) records or customs declarations.

If you encounter any of these signs, pause the negotiation and initiate a full background check immediately. Our team at GoCNScout recommends using our supplier database to cross-reference the company’s trade activity.

Checking Corporate Registration Status via Official Databases

The first step in any verification process is to confirm the company’s legal existence. China’s official corporate registry is the National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System (NECIPS), managed by the State Administration for Market Regulation. You can search for a company by its full Chinese name or unified social credit code. This database will show you:

  • Registration status – Active, revoked, or canceled.
  • Registered capital – A higher registered capital often indicates a more established business.
  • Business scope – Ensure the company is legally allowed to manufacture or trade the products you need.
  • Legal representative – Cross-check this name with the person you are communicating with.

We recommend always starting here. It is a free, government-run database that provides a baseline of legitimacy. However, it does not show export performance or factory capacity—that is where our platform comes in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I verify a Chinese company for free?

Yes, partially. You can use the official NECIPS database (gsxt.gov.cn) to check a company’s registration status, legal representative, and business scope for free. However, free checks do not include export records, financial health, or factory audit reports. For a complete picture, we recommend using a paid service like GoCNScout’s export database to access customs data.

What is the official registry database in China?

The official corporate registry in China is the National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System (NECIPS), accessible at gsxt.gov.cn. This is a government-run platform that provides basic registration data for all legally registered companies in China. It is the most authoritative source for confirming a company’s legal status.

How do I know if a supplier is a real factory?

To confirm a supplier is a genuine manufacturer, you need to verify three things: (1) their business license includes “manufacturing” in the scope, (2) they have a verifiable physical factory address (use Google Maps or a local agent), and (3) they have a consistent history of exporting goods that match their product line.
